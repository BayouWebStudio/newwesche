The Transition

Same geometry,
different surface.

A few years ago I started painting on canvas. Same obsession with sacred geometry — Platonic solids, mandalas, the Flower of Life — but without the constraints of skin. On canvas it can be as large as I want. As detailed as I want. Without a client having to sit through twelve hours of it.

The paintings are still early work. I'm figuring out what this version of the geometry wants to be. But the underlying thing is the same — lines placed with intention, forms that feel like they mean something even if I can't say exactly what.

More work being added throughout 2026. The transition from skin to canvas is still in progress.

Medium

Acrylic on Canvas

Geometric forms rendered in matte and gloss acrylics on stretched linen canvas. Some pieces incorporate gold leaf.

Scale

Large Format

Most paintings range from 36" × 36" to 60" × 72". The geometry wants room — small formats compromise the precision.

The Process

From Skin
to Canvas.

Everything I was doing on skin for a decade — the same precision, the same geometric obsession — now has room to breathe. On canvas I can work at a scale where the relationships between forms are fully visible. Where the mathematics can unfold the way they want to.

I'm not a painter who happened to learn geometry. I'm a geometer who happened to find a larger surface. The approach is the same: intention first, then precision, then patience. The canvas doesn't move under the tool, but the forms still have to be earned.

The paintings are early work — most of them made in the last two years. I'm showing them because the underlying thing is real, not because I think they're finished. More work coming throughout 2026.

Materials

01

Stretched Linen Canvas

Heavy-weight Belgian linen, gallery-depth bars. Most pieces are 1.5" depth to read as objects on the wall, not just pictures.

02

Professional Acrylics

Heavy-body artist's acrylics, primarily in monochromatic palettes with occasional saturated accent. Matte medium, occasional gloss finish for specific forms.

03

Gold Leaf & Metallic

Select pieces incorporate 22k gold leaf or genuine metallic leaf on key geometric nodes. Applied over multiple layers of isolation coat.

04

Geometric Drafting

Every piece begins as a technical drawing — the same compass-and-straightedge approach I use for tattoo design, scaled up. No digital printing, no projectors.
