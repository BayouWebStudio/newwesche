The methodology

Most LLM leaderboards saturate — every decent model clusters at 88–97 and the ranking stops meaning anything. This eval is built to discriminate and to be verifiable:

  • 10 domains across capability (tool-use, instruction-following, structured output, coding, reasoning, long-context, faithfulness, visual generation) and calibration (safety, robustness).
  • Deterministic grading — executed unit tests, JSON-schema validation, exact tool-argument matching, needle retrieval. No LLM-judge to game.
  • Reliability — every scenario runs K times; consistency is scored, so flaky models bleed points.
  • Capability vs calibration — scored separately, penalizing both over- and under-refusal.
  • Efficiency — useful-token ratio, penalizing overthinking.
  • An adversarial hard tier (override-needles, stacked constraints, cognitive traps, edge-case coding) that pulls strong models off the ceiling.

The hardware

  • 4× NVIDIA DGX Spark (GB10 Grace-Blackwell, 128 GB unified each), RoCE fabric.
  • MiMo V2.5 Omni — 310B/15B-active NVFP4, virtual-head TP=3 across 3 Sparks, 1M context, omnimodal.
  • DeepSeek V4 Flash — sparse-MLA, 2 Sparks, 1M context, reasoning-default.
  • Qwopus 3.6-27B — llama.cpp MTP2 self-speculation, single Spark, 256K context.
